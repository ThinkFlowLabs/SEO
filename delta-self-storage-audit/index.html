02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild. Together they explain why a #1 local listing produces only a #5 organic result.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · SCHEMA

No LocalBusiness or SelfStorage schema on a two-location facility.

Impact: Invisible to the structured-data signals that drive the local pack, rich results and AI citations.

The homepage ships Organization, WebSite and Product JSON-LD, but no SelfStorage / LocalBusiness markup and no FAQPage. For a business with two physical addresses (694 US-50 and 1044 Palmer St), each location should emit its own SelfStorage entity with address, geo, hours, phone and amenityFeature. This is the strongest single signal you can send Google and the AI engines, and it is the reason Claude Sonnet 4.6 (which leans on structured data) cited you only once in five.

Fix: Add a @graph with two SelfStorage LocalBusiness nodes (one per address) + an Organization node, plus FAQPage on the homepage and FAQ page. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: 1 dev day. Expected lift: local-pack reinforcement + AI-citation eligibility, especially on Claude.
2
CRITICAL · REVIEWS

Only 3 Google reviews. Your nearest rival has 46.

Impact: Reviews drive both the local pack ranking and the "best storage in Delta" AI answers, where you currently rank 3rd-4th.

The Hwy 50 location shows a 5.0 rating from just 3 reviews. Allsafe Storage has 46, StoreSafely has 10. In a town this size, review count is the deciding factor in both Google's local ranking and the way Claude/GPT/Gemini order their "best storage" lists, they lead with the facility that has the most social proof. You have the rating; you are missing the volume.

Fix: Launch a review drive: SMS/email every move-in with a one-tap Google review link, add a QR code at both gates and the counter, and target 25+ reviews per location in 90 days. This is the highest-ROI move on the entire list. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
3
CRITICAL · CORE WEB VITALS

Mobile LCP is 3.2s and layout shift (CLS 0.233) fails Core Web Vitals.

Impact: Lighthouse mobile Performance = 0.68. Two of three Core Web Vitals fail; mobile is most of local storage search.

DataForSEO Lighthouse (mobile): Performance 0.68, with LCP = 3.2s (Google's "Good" threshold is <2.5s) and CLS = 0.233 (threshold <0.1, this is visible content jumping as the page loads). First Contentful Paint (0.6s) and Total Blocking Time (0ms) are excellent, so the fixes are narrow: the hero image and unreserved layout space.

Fix: Serve the hero/LCP image with explicit width/height + fetchpriority="high", reserve space for any late-loading elements (banners, embeds) to kill the layout shift, and preload the hero. Eta: 1-2 dev days. Target: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, Perf > 0.85.
4
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

The site is too thin to rank organically, and has no location landing pages.

Impact: 358-word homepage, ~8 ranked keywords, no per-location or service pages to capture "rv storage", "climate", "cheap" intent.

The homepage runs 358 words with 20 internal links. There is one combined /facilities page rather than a dedicated, indexable landing page per address, and no pages targeting the obvious modifiers (RV/boat parking, drive-up, business storage, "cheap storage Delta"). Aggregators like Sparefoot and SelfStorage.com outrank you organically precisely because they publish more structured, deeper Delta content than you do about your own facility.

Fix: Build a real landing page per location (Hwy 50, Palmer St) with address, map, hours, unit table, amenities and FAQ, plus one "storage unit size guide for Delta" hub. Promote /size-guide (currently pos 94) with internal links. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
5
HIGH · ON-PAGE / SOCIAL

No og:image, and 4 of 6 homepage images have no alt text.

Impact: Broken link previews when the page is shared; weak image-accessibility and image-search signals.

The homepage declares twitter:card = summary but ships no og:image, so shares on Facebook, iMessage and WhatsApp render with no thumbnail. Separately, 4 of 6 images carry no alt attribute, which costs accessibility points and image-search visibility for a business whose units are inherently visual.

Fix: Add a branded og:image (1200x630) sitewide and descriptive, location-specific alt text on every image (e.g. "drive-up storage units at 694 US-50, Delta CO"). Eta: 2-3 hours.
Bottom line: this is a strong business on a thin website. Unlike most audits, the foundation (titles, metas, canonical, H1) is already solid. The wins here are additive, schema, reviews, content depth and a Core Web Vitals pass, and they are the difference between owning the map and owning the entire search result.
