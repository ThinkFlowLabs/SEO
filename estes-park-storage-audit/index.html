02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild, and none is a speed problem. Together they explain why a business that dominates locally still leaves AI citations and the "cost" answer on the table.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · SCHEMA

Only WebSite schema. No SelfStorage, LocalBusiness or FAQ markup at all.

Impact: Invisible to the structured-data signals that drive the local pack, rich results and AI citations, and no rival has it either.

The homepage ships a single WebSite JSON-LD block, and nothing else, no SelfStorage / LocalBusiness markup and no FAQPage. For a gated, paved, drive-up facility with 24/7 access at 1210 Woodstock Dr, the page should emit a SelfStorage entity with address, geo, hours, phone, amenityFeature and the 4.6-star / 55-review aggregateRating. No competitor in the Estes Park SERP publishes this, so it is a pure, uncontested differentiator, and it is the likely reason Claude Sonnet 4.6 (which leans on structured data) cited you only twice in five.

Fix: Add a SelfStorage LocalBusiness node (1210 Woodstock Dr) with geo, hours, amenityFeature and aggregateRating, plus an Organization node and FAQPage on the homepage and an /faq hub. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: 1 dev day. Expected lift: local-pack reinforcement + AI-citation eligibility, especially on Claude.
2
CRITICAL · TITLE / META

The title is literally "Self Storage Units" with no brand, no city, no og:image.

Impact: The single most-weighted on-page ranking and click signal is a platform default; shares render blank.

The homepage <title> is the generic platform string "Self Storage Units", with no brand and no city, and the meta description is the unmodified template default ("Find secure self storage near you..."). There is also no og:image, so shares on Facebook, iMessage and WhatsApp render with no thumbnail. You rank #1 in spite of this; a real, city-and-brand title plus a written meta would harden that #1 and lift click-through, and it is a fix no competitor has bothered to make.

Fix: Rewrite to Self Storage Units in Estes Park, CO | Estes Park Storage with a written meta naming gated, drive-up, 24/7 access and the RMNP gateway, and add a branded 1200x630 og:image sitewide. Eta: 1 hour.
3
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

A ~199-word homepage, and no service or seasonal landing pages.

Impact: 199-word homepage, ~17 ranked keywords (mostly the 6 head terms), no pages to capture drive-up, RV/recreation, cost or RMNP-gateway intent.

The homepage runs only ~199 words, below the threshold where Google and AI engines have enough text to quote. There are no dedicated, indexable landing pages for the obvious modifiers, drive-up units, RV/recreation-gear storage, off-season RMNP visitor storage, or a transparent pricing page. The competitors that AI surfaces on the seasonal and cost prompts (Woodstock, Alpine, and pricing via inselfstorage) win precisely because they publish more structured, deeper Estes Park content than you do about your own facility.

Fix: Expand the homepage past ~600 words (drive-up, gated + paved, 24/7 access, "minutes from Rocky Mountain National Park"), then add lightweight service pages: drive-up storage, RV/recreation-gear storage and a seasonal/off-season RMNP angle, each with a unit table and FAQ. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
4
HIGH · PRICING / AI COST GAP

No on-page pricing, so the "cost" AI prompt returns 0 of 4.

Impact: P4 ("how much does a unit cost in Estes Park") was cited by zero of four models; competitors with published rates win it.

In the flagship AI test, the cost prompt ("How much does a storage unit cost in Estes Park, CO?") returned 0 of 4 citations, the only prompt where you score nothing. The reason is structural: there is no pricing anywhere on the site for an engine to quote. When Perplexity needed a number it pulled live rates from inselfstorage ($119 5x10 / $249 10x10 / $375 10x20) instead of from you. Publishing your own rate table both closes the AI gap and captures the "storage cost Estes Park" search.

Fix: Publish a transparent rate table (5x5 through 10x20+) on a /prices or /units page, mirror the same numbers in an FAQ answer and Offer/priceRange schema. Eta: half a day. Target: move P4 from 0/4 toward 3/4.
5
HIGH · AI / CLAUDE BLIND SPOT

Claude cites you only 2 of 5, tied directly to the thin, unstructured site.

Impact: Claude Sonnet 4.6 leans on directories and structured data and under-weights your thin site, so you lose the channel you most easily own.

Across the 5x4 grid, Gemini, GPT-5.5 and Sonar each cited you 4 of 5 times, but Claude Sonnet 4.6 cited you only twice. Claude leans hardest on aggregator/directory data (namesandnumbers, SpareFoot, MoversCorp) and structured markup, and it under-weights a 199-word page with no LocalBusiness schema. This is not a Claude problem, it is a symptom of findings 1 to 4: the same schema, content depth and pricing fixes that help Google directly recover the Claude channel.

Fix: Ship the SelfStorage + FAQPage schema (finding 1), expand the homepage and publish pricing (findings 3-4), and keep NAP identical across the directories Claude reads. Eta: folds into the above. Target: Claude 2/5 → 4/5 on the 90-day re-run.
Bottom line: this is a dominant business on the weakest website in its market. Performance is already healthy, so none of these fixes touch speed, they are additive: schema, a real title, content depth, an on-page price table and the RMNP-gateway angle. No local competitor has any of them, so capturing them locks in the lead and extends it to every AI engine.
