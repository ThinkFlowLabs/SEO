1
CRITICAL · AI VISIBILITY

Near-total AI invisibility: cited in just 1 of 20 flagship answers.

Impact: AI search is becoming the default for "near me" storage discovery, and you are absent from nineteen of twenty answers while rivals own them.

Across Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.5 and Sonar-Reasoning-Pro, Moriches Storage was cited exactly once in 20 calls, by Gemini, on the pricing prompt, and only because your site publishes a concrete rate table ($135 for a 5x5 up to $425 for a 10x20). For "best," "how to rent," "drive-up," and "cheap," you do not appear under any name, while East Moriches Self Storage and StorQuest are cited in nearly every answer. The lone citation is the proof point: publishing concrete, crawlable data earns citations. You have done it once, by accident, on price. Do it deliberately across cost, access, size and FAQ content and the citation rate climbs.

Fix: Publish concrete, quotable content the way your pricing already is: a cost page with the full rate table, an FAQ page answering the exact test prompts in plain prose, and "best / cheap / drive-up" framed copy. Add FAQPage schema (Section 06). Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev. Expected lift: from 1/20 toward 8+/20 AI citations.
2
CRITICAL · NAP DUPLICATION

Your own legacy identity, "Storage R Us East," outranks your real domain.

Impact: Authority, links and citations are split across two identities for one facility, and the legacy one is winning the organic slot you should hold.

"Storage R Us East" (storageruseast.com) is an older identity for this same facility, it carries the same phone (631-874-5306) and the same 192 Frowein Rd address, and it has its own Facebook page. In the live East Moriches SERP it ranks organic #5 while your real domain, storagemoriches.com, does not appear in the organic top 14 at all. Google's "people also search for" even surfaces "storage r." This is not a competitor, it is your past self splitting your NAP signals, your link equity and your citations across two names. Gemini and Perplexity even reference the legacy name instead of your current one.

Fix: Consolidate the identity. 301-redirect storageruseast.com to storagemoriches.com, unify the Google Business Profile and every directory citation under the current name and NAP, and update or merge the legacy Facebook page. Eta: 1 day + citation cleanup. Expected lift: recovers the organic authority currently parked on the legacy domain.
3
CRITICAL · ON-PAGE

Missing meta description, no og:image, no FAQ schema, brand-only H1.

Impact: The page cannot describe itself in search results or social shares, cannot earn FAQ rich results, and does not target its own head keyword in the H1.

The technical base is genuinely good, rich SelfStorage schema and a correct self-referential canonical are already in place. But four on-page basics are missing. The meta description is absent entirely (Lighthouse confirms no value), so Google writes its own snippet and the SEO score is dinged to 85. There is no og:image, so shares on Facebook, iMessage and WhatsApp render blank. There is no FAQPage schema, the exact format AI engines extract. And the H1 is just "Moriches Storage", a brand label rather than a keyword target like "Self Storage in East Moriches, NY."

Fix: Add a 150-160 char meta description (Section 03), a branded 1200x630 og:image sitewide, FAQPage JSON-LD on the homepage and a /faq hub, and rewrite the H1 to "Self Storage in East Moriches, NY." Eta: 2-3 hours.
4
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

Zero ranked keywords on a thin ~200-word page with no service pages.

Impact: The domain is organically invisible: no ranked terms, no page in the organic top 14, no pages targeting drive-up, cost, cheap or RV intent.

DataForSEO Labs indexes zero ranked keywords for the domain, and storagemoriches.com is absent from the organic SERP for its own head term. The site is a single thin Wix page (roughly 200 words of raw content) with no dedicated, indexable landing pages for the obvious local modifiers: drive-up storage, 24/7 access, cost, cheap storage, or RV/vehicle storage. Aggregators like StorageCafe and Yelp, and rivals like East Moriches Self Storage, take the organic slots for your own town precisely because they publish more structured local content than you do about your own facility.

Fix: Build lightweight, schema-rich landing pages for the core intents (drive-up, cost, cheap, 24/7 access, RV/vehicle) plus a size guide, and expand the homepage past 500 words with a unit table, amenities and FAQ. Even 300-400 words each will outrank the aggregators on these low-difficulty local terms. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
5
HIGH · REVIEWS / COMPETITION

Lowest-rated in the pack (4.1/18) while facing national chains.

Impact: Reviews drive both local-pack rank and the "best storage" AI answers, and you are up against StorQuest and CubeSmart, not just local independents.

You hold local-pack #2, but with the lowest rating and volume in the pack: 4.1 from 18 reviews, behind East Moriches Self Storage (5.0/3) and StorQuest (4.6/31). In a market where AI engines and Google both lead with the most-reviewed, best-rated facility, that gap is why you are listed below rivals or not at all. And unlike a typical small market, you face national-brand competition: StorQuest sits on the same road (10 Frowein Rd, Center Moriches) and CubeSmart covers Riverhead and Patchogue, both with bigger sites and review bases.

Fix: Launch a review drive: SMS/email every move-in a one-tap Google review link, add a QR code at the gate and counter, and target 40+ reviews above a 4.5 rating in 90 days to clear StorQuest. Lean into what the chains lack: locally owned, 24/7 drive-up, concrete published pricing. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
Bottom line: this is a real, fast facility that is simply not visible. The foundation (schema, canonical, page speed) is already solid, which is rare. The wins here are additive and cheap, consolidate the duplicate identity, publish content AI can quote, add the missing tags, and drive reviews, and they are the difference between riding one map listing and owning storage search in East Moriches.

THE PROOF THAT DATA WORKS

One citation, and it tells you exactly what to do next.

The single time any model cited you, Gemini 3.1 Pro on the pricing prompt, it quoted your published rate table almost verbatim: "5x5 Drive-Up: ~$135/month ... 10x20 Drive-Up: ~$425/month," sourced directly to storagemoriches.com. It cited you for the one thing your site states concretely and crawlably.

That is the whole strategy in one data point. AI engines quote concrete, crawlable facts. Right now you publish exactly one (price), so you earn exactly one citation. Publish the same way on access, size, drive-up and FAQ, add FAQPage schema, and consolidate your identity, and you move from 1 of 20 toward 8+ of 20, on a domain that ranks for nothing today.

Why this matters now

AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews) is displacing a growing share of "near me" storage discovery. The four models tested are exactly what powers those surfaces. You are effectively absent, and a national chain on your own road plus the local incumbent are taking every answer. This is the gap with the most room to close in the entire portfolio.

What earns citations

  1. Concrete, crawlable content, the rate table already earned your one citation; do the same for access, size and amenities.
  2. FAQPage schema answering cost, access and size in plain prose, the format LLMs extract.
  3. A consolidated identity, redirect Storage R Us East so engines find one authoritative source, not two weak ones.
  4. Review volume past StorQuest's 31, AI lists lead with the best-rated, most-reviewed facility.
  5. Service + price pages the engines can quote directly for "cost," "drive-up" and "cheap" prompts.
  6. An /faq hub mirroring the exact prompts above so every model finds a quotable answer.

90-day LLM target

Re-run this same 5x4 grid in 90 days. Move from 1 of 20 to 8+ of 20, with the goal of appearing in the "best" and "cheap" answers, not just on price. Capturing a second and third model (Claude and GPT-5.5, currently 0/5 each) is the clearest single KPI.
