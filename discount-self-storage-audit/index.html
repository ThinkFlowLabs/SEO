02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild. Together they explain why a four-location network with the best reviews in town still sits behind a single rival at #1.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · SCHEMA

No LocalBusiness or SelfStorage schema across a four-location network.

Impact: Invisible to the structured-data signals that drive the local pack, rich results and AI citations, for all four facilities at once.

The homepage ships WebSite, Organization and two City JSON-LD nodes, but no SelfStorage / LocalBusiness markup and no FAQPage. For a business with four physical addresses, Airport (500 S Boulevard St, Gunnison), North (1825 CO-135, Gunnison, outdoor RV/boat), Crested Butte (246 Buckley Dr, daily snow removal, ground-level) and Riverland (407 Riverland Dr, interior upstairs), each location should emit its own SelfStorage entity with address, geo, hours, phone and amenityFeature. This is the strongest single signal you can send Google and the AI engines, and it is the most likely reason Claude declined to cite you on the pricing prompt.

Fix: Add a @graph with four SelfStorage LocalBusiness nodes (one per address) plus an Organization parent, and FAQPage on the homepage and a /faq hub. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: 1 dev day. Expected lift: local-pack reinforcement across all four sites + AI-citation eligibility, especially on Claude.
2
CRITICAL · CORE WEB VITALS / RENDERING

CLS 0.171 fails Core Web Vitals, the H1 is JS-only, and the meta has a hidden zero-width space.

Impact: Layout shift hurts mobile ranking eligibility, and an invisible (server-side) H1 plus a malformed meta weaken the two most basic on-page signals.

DataForSEO Lighthouse (mobile): Performance 83 with LCP 1.7s (good) but CLS = 0.171 (Google's threshold is <0.1, this is visible content jumping as the page loads). Two further raw-HTML issues compound it: the H1 is injected by JavaScript only ("Store with Discount Self Storage in the Gunnison Valley!" never appears in the server HTML, so crawlers that do not execute JS see no H1), and the meta description begins with a stray zero-width space (U+200B) that can corrupt how the snippet is parsed. FCP (0.3s) and TBT (10ms) are excellent, so the fixes are narrow.

Fix: Reserve layout space for late-loading elements (banners, the Duda hero, embeds) to push CLS under 0.1; output a real server-rendered <h1> in the page markup; and strip the leading U+200B from the meta description. Eta: half a day. Target: CLS < 0.1, a crawlable H1, clean meta.
3
CRITICAL · LOCAL / COMPETITIVE

Gunnison Secure Storage outranks you despite your stronger reviews.

Impact: You hold the best review profile in town yet sit behind one rival at both local-pack #1 and organic #1, a GBP-optimization and content-depth gap.

In the live local pack, Gunnison Secure Storage takes #1 (4.3★, 6 reviews) while your North location sits at #2 (4.7★, 34 reviews) and Airport at #3. Organically the same pattern holds, gunnisonsecurestorage.com at #1, your homepage at #2. You have far more, and better, reviews, so the gap is not social proof, it is on-page content depth and Google Business Profile completeness. A facility with more reviews that still ranks second is leaving the #1 slot on the table.

Fix: Close the GBP gap (complete categories, services, photos, Q&A, attributes on all four listings) and ship deeper per-location content so each address has its own indexable page with reviews embedded. Surfacing your 34-review North profile properly should flip the pack. Eta: half a day GBP + 2-3 days content.
4
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

Brand-dependent, thin keyword footprint with no per-location or per-service pages.

Impact: ~249-word homepage, ranks only for brand and "discount storage" terms; non-brand local/service intent (Crested Butte, RV, snow, student) is unranked.

The homepage runs about 249 words. The domain ranks #1-5 essentially only for brand and "discount storage" terms plus gunnison co storage units (#1), and is otherwise absent for the obvious non-brand local and service queries: Crested Butte storage, RV/boat storage Gunnison, climate/snow storage, Western Colorado University student storage. There is no dedicated, indexable page per location or per service, so the four-location network, your biggest structural advantage, is invisible to those searches. Gunnison Secure Storage and aggregators (RentCafe, StorageCafe, SpareFoot) capture that intent because they publish more depth.

Fix: Build a real landing page per location (Airport, North RV/boat, Crested Butte snow-managed, Riverland interior) plus service pages for RV/boat, climate/snow and student storage. Each with address, map, hours, unit table, amenities and FAQ. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
5
HIGH · PRICING / AI

No transparent pricing, the one thing keeping you from 20 of 20 AI citations.

Impact: Claude declined to cite you on the pure "how much does a unit cost" prompt and fell back to aggregator data; published rates would close the gap.

Across the 5x4 AI test, Discount was cited 19 of 20 times. The single miss was Claude on the pricing query ("How much does a storage unit cost in Gunnison, CO?"), where it leaned on aggregator pricing data instead of your site, because your site publishes no per-unit rates. Gemini, GPT and Sonar still cited you on that prompt; Perplexity even recited your features verbatim ("24/7 access, drive-up units, RV/boat parking, LED lighting, extra-high ceilings"). The only thing missing from a perfect score is a quotable price table.

Fix: Publish a transparent pricing page (per-unit-size rate bands per location) and mirror it in Offer/AggregateOffer schema and a pricing FAQ. This directly answers the one prompt you lose and should take you to a clean 20/20. Eta: half a day once rates are confirmed.
Bottom line: this is a strong business on a thin website, and unusually, the AI engines already treat you as the default answer. The foundation (titles, canonical, og:image, healthy LCP) is mostly solid. The wins are additive, per-location schema, a content layer, a CWV/H1/meta cleanup and published pricing, and they are the difference between trailing one rival and owning the entire valley.
