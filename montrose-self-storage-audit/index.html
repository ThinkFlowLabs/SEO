Executive Summary

The situation in 60 seconds.

Montrose Self Storage is in a genuinely strong position, #1 in the Google local pack with 60 reviews and cited in 13 of 20 flagship-AI answers, sitting on top of a thin, structurally invisible website. A handful of fixes (schema, title/meta, a pricing page, content depth, consolidating a duplicate domain) would convert that local strength into durable page-one organic rankings and a default-first AI citation.

  1. You win the map, but lose the organic click. You hold the #1 local-pack slot for "self storage Montrose CO" yet the first organic result is only #6, behind A Storage Place at #4 and behind directories (Yelp, U-Haul) that monetise your own town. Northside Storage and A Storage Place are the brands to beat.
  2. AI already knows you, 13 of 20. Across Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.5 and Sonar-Reasoning-Pro, the facility was cited 13 times in 20 calls, and GPT-5.5 even named it "best overall". But Gemini cited you in only one of five prompts, and every "how much does it cost" prompt missed you entirely (0 of 4).
  3. The site ships zero schema and a generic 18-character title. No SelfStorage, LocalBusiness or FAQPage JSON-LD anywhere, a <title> of just "Self Storage Units" with no city or brand, a generic meta description, and no og:image. This is the single biggest fixable gap.
  4. A second domain is splitting your SEO equity. A second domain resolves to this same 239 N 9th St facility, and AI engines and Google treat it as a separate source, so the authority that should compound on one brand is divided across two.
  5. Most of the upside is unlocked by ~6 fixes, not a rebuild. Lighthouse is excellent (Perf 0.95, LCP 1.2s, CLS 0), so performance is not the problem. The gaps are schema, a real title/meta, a pricing page, content depth, reviews quality and domain consolidation.

Our verdict

Montrose Self Storage has done the hard part, it owns the map, has real review volume and a fast, healthy site. What it is missing is the structured-data layer, the on-page geo signals, a published-price page and the consolidation of a split domain that together turn a strong local listing into the default organic and AI answer for storage in Montrose.

Ship LocalBusiness + FAQ schema, a real geo-rich title/meta and an og:image in 30 days. Publish a pricing/size page and consolidate the duplicate domain via 301s in 60 days. 02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild, and none is about speed (the site is genuinely fast). Together they explain why a #1 local listing produces only a #6 first organic result.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · SCHEMA + ON-PAGE

Zero schema, an 18-character title, a generic meta and no og:image.

Impact: Invisible to the structured-data signals that drive the local pack, rich results and AI citations, and a wasted homepage title for the head term.

The homepage ships no JSON-LD at all, no SelfStorage, no LocalBusiness, no Organization, no FAQPage. On top of that, the <title> is literally "Self Storage Units" (18 characters, no city, no brand), the meta description is the generic "Find secure self storage near you..." with no "Montrose" or "CO", and there is no og:image (shares render blank). For a business that already wins the map, this is the single biggest missed signal for Google and the AI engines, and it is why Gemini 3.1 Pro (which leans on structured data) cited you in only one of five prompts.

Fix: Add a @graph with a SelfStorage / LocalBusiness node (239 N 9th St, geo, hours, phone, amenityFeature) + FAQPage on the homepage. Rewrite the title to Self Storage Units in Montrose, CO | Montrose Self Storage and the meta to a geo-rich line. Add a 1200x630 og:image. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: 1 dev day.
2
CRITICAL · DUPLICATE DOMAIN

A second domain points to the same facility and splits your SEO equity.

Impact: Authority, links and AI citations are divided across two domains for one physical facility, so neither ranks as strongly as a consolidated brand would.

A second domain currently resolves to this same 239 N 9th St facility. Google and the AI engines treat it as a separate source, so the link equity, content and citations that should compound on a single brand are split in two. In the AI test, the climate-control and 24/7 content that Claude and GPT-5.5 quote most often lives on that second domain rather than on montroseselfstorage.com, which means the wins are landing on the wrong address. This is pure leakage: the same effort would rank far better concentrated on one canonical brand.

Fix: Consolidate all signals onto montroseselfstorage.com via 301 redirects from the second domain, plus a single self-canonical sitewide. Migrate any unique climate-control / 24/7 / pricing content from the second domain onto this one before redirecting so no quotable content is lost. Eta: 1 dev day + content port.
3
CRITICAL · AI / PRICING GAP

Gemini is a blind spot (1/5) and no model can answer "how much does it cost" (0/4).

Impact: You miss the entire pricing-intent AI surface, and the one model most tied to structured data barely sees you.

In the flagship 5x4 grid, Gemini 3.1 Pro cited the facility in only one of five prompts, while Claude, GPT-5.5 and Sonar each cited you four times. And the "How much does a storage unit cost in Montrose, CO?" prompt returned nothing for any model (0 of 4), because no published rates or cost content exists on the site. No facility in Montrose owns that pricing answer, so it is an uncontested query sitting on the table. Gemini's miss tracks directly to the absent schema from finding #1.

Fix: Publish a transparent unit-size-and-price page (5x5 to 10x30 bands, drive-up vs interior) with FAQPage + Offer schema and a plain-prose "how much does storage cost in Montrose" answer. Combined with the schema rollout, this is what closes both the Gemini gap and the 0/4 pricing miss. Eta: 1 day copy + dev.
4
HIGH · ORGANIC / KEYWORD FOCUS

First organic result is #6 behind A Storage Place, and 26% of keywords are out-of-market.

Impact: You concede the organic click to a rival and a directory, while a quarter of your ranked terms point at Front Range cities you cannot serve.

You win the local pack (#1) but your first organic result is only #6, behind A Storage Place at #4 and ahead of Northside only slightly. Worse, of ~35 ranked keywords, 9 (26%) are out-of-market Front Range terms, Boulder, Castle Rock, Parker, Red Rocks, that the facility cannot serve and ranks only p8-11 for. Those are the highest-volume terms in the profile, so the site is bleeding relevance to statewide queries while core Montrose money terms sit at #11 to #65. The on-page geo signal is diffuse, not Montrose-focused.

Fix: Tighten on-page geo toward Montrose plus genuinely-served neighbours (Delta, Olathe, Ridgway); stop optimising for statewide "Colorado" terms. Build a dedicated, schema-rich page per facility and a Montrose-anchored homepage so the core terms (#11-#65) climb. Eta: 2-3 days.
5
HIGH · CONTENT + REVIEWS

A 262-word homepage and the lowest local-pack rating (4.4 vs 4.6 / 5.0).

Impact: Too thin to rank organically, and a review-quality gap that AI "best storage" lists and the local pack both reward.

The homepage runs only ~262 words (DataForSEO flagged low_content_rate) with just two images, neither carrying alt text. And while you have real review volume (60), your 4.4 rating is the lowest of the three local-pack winners, Northside Storage sits at 4.6 (78 reviews) and 550 Storage at a perfect 5.0 (54). Rating, not just count, is what tips both Google's local ranking and the way AI engines order their "best storage" answers.

Fix: Expand the homepage to 600+ words with a unit table, amenities, service-area and FAQ, add location-specific alt text to every image, and launch a review-quality drive (one-tap links at move-in, gate QR codes, fast resolution of any low scores) to lift the 4.4 toward 4.7+. Eta: 2-3 days copy + ongoing review work.
Bottom line: this is a strong business on a thin, invisible website. Unlike most audits, performance is not the problem, the site is genuinely fast (Perf 0.95, LCP 1.2s, CLS 0). The wins here are additive: schema, a real title/meta, a pricing page, domain consolidation and content depth, and they are the difference between owning the map and owning the entire search result.
