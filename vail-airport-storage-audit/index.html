Executive Summary

The situation in 60 seconds.

Vail Airport Storage has the rare problem of a near-perfect website that is not yet winning classic local search. The site is cited in 17 of 20 flagship-AI answers, has best-in-class schema and Core Web Vitals, yet it is absent from the Gypsum local pack and ranks only #5 organically. The fix is not engineering: it is GBP, reviews, dedicated location pages and pointing the keyword profile at local commercial intent instead of national how-to traffic.

  1. The website is excellent, but classic local organic is weak. Rich SelfStorage + FAQPage schema, a clean keyword-rich H1, an og:image, a self-canonical and Lighthouse mobile Perf 0.95 / LCP 1.2s / CLS 0. Despite all of that you are not in the Gypsum local pack and rank only #5 organically for "self storage Gypsum CO", behind StorageMart, Gypsum Self Storage, STOR-N-LOCK and Gypsum Mini Storage.
  2. The keyword profile is mis-aimed. Your strongest rankings are national informational head terms ("prices of self storage units", "self storage units with electricity", "storage size guide") driven by blog content. The high-intent local commercial terms (Avon, Edwards, Eagle, Gypsum) rank only #13 to #28. You are winning traffic that does not convert and losing the searches that do.
  3. Reviews and GBP are the top growth lever. StorageMart shows ~306 Google reviews and owns the Gypsum 3-pack; most rivals sit at 12 to 34. Review volume and a fully optimized Google Business Profile are the single biggest reasons you are out of the local pack.
  4. There are no dedicated Vail, Avon, Edwards or Eagle location pages. The site speaks to the whole valley from one homepage, so it has nothing indexable to rank for the up-valley commercial searches that AAA Self Storage, StorageMart and Extra Space currently own.
  5. AI already loves you, 17 of 20. Gemini and Perplexity cite you in all five prompts and often rank you #1 on airport, climate and Gypsum queries. The one soft spot is GPT-5.5 (3/5), which drops you on down-valley Vail/Avon/Edwards questions.

Our verdict

Vail Airport Storage has done what most facilities never do, it built a fast, schema-rich, AI-friendly site. What it has not done is convert that into local commercial visibility. The map pack, the reviews, and the location-specific pages are all missing, so the strongest building in the valley for AI is invisible in the Gypsum 3-pack.

Optimize both the GBP and launch a review drive in 30 days. Build dedicated Gypsum, Vail, Avon, Edwards and Eagle location pages and re-point the content toward local commercial intent in 60 days. Earn local citations and compound reviews in 90 days. We project a move from #5 to a top-3 organic baseline, entry into the Gypsum local pack, and from 17 of 20 to a near-perfect AI-citation rate, this time as the #1 pick down-valley too.

02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. Crucially, none of them is a technical defect, the engineering is already best-in-class. They explain why a perfect site sits at #5 organically and outside the Gypsum local pack.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · LOCAL VISIBILITY

A near-perfect site that is absent from the Gypsum local pack and only #5 organically.

Impact: The map pack and the top organic slots, where most local storage clicks happen, are going to StorageMart and three Gypsum rivals instead of you.

The technical foundation is excellent: rich SelfStorage + FAQPage schema, a single keyword-rich H1, an og:image, a self-canonical, and Lighthouse mobile Perf 0.95, LCP 1.2s, CLS 0. Yet for "self storage Gypsum CO" you rank organic #5 (absolute #11) and you are not in the 3-pack at all, which is led by StorageMart, then Gypsum Self Storage and STOR-N-LOCK. This is not an engineering problem. It is a local-relevance and Google Business Profile problem: the signals Google uses to build the map pack (GBP completeness, reviews, proximity-relevant landing content) are the ones you are under-sending.

Fix: Treat the GBP as the primary asset: complete every field, correct primary/secondary categories, add fresh geo-tagged photos, post weekly, seed Q&A, and connect it to a Gypsum location page. Pair it with the review drive in Finding 3. Eta: GBP half a day, then ongoing. Expected lift: entry into the Gypsum 3-pack and movement from #5 toward the top 3 organically.
2
CRITICAL · KEYWORD FOCUS

The keyword profile is mis-aimed: national how-to terms instead of local commercial intent.

Impact: You rank for high-volume informational searches nationwide that rarely convert, while the local commercial terms that fill units rank #13 to #28.

Your strongest rankings are national informational head terms surfaced by blog and guide content: prices of self storage units (#3), self storage units with electricity (#5), average cost of self storage units (#6), storage size guide (#9). Useful for authority, but these visitors are scattered across the country and do not rent a Gypsum unit. Meanwhile the terms that do convert sit on page 2 to 3: storage unit avon (#13), self storage avon (#16), avon storage (#25), eagle self storage (#23). The site is winning the wrong race.

Fix: Keep the informational content as a supporting cluster, but build and internally link a dedicated commercial layer targeting "storage units gypsum / vail valley / avon / edwards / eagle". Localize titles and H1s, and route internal links from the high-traffic guides to the local money pages. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
3
CRITICAL · REVIEWS / GBP

Review volume trails the market leader badly, and reviews drive both the pack and AI answers.

Impact: StorageMart shows ~306 Google reviews and owns the Gypsum 3-pack; most rivals sit at 12 to 34. Review count is the deciding factor you are not pulling.

In the Gypsum local pack, StorageMart (4.8, ~306 reviews) sits at #1, with New Frontier Self Storage (~34) and Gypsum Self Storage (~12) filling the other two slots. Review volume is one of the strongest local-pack ranking factors and it is also how Claude, GPT, Gemini and Perplexity order their "best storage" lists, they lead with the most-reviewed, most-socially-proven facility. With a strong site but a thin review count, you are well-built but under-trusted in the eyes of both Google and the AI engines.

Fix: Launch a review drive: SMS/email every move-in with a one-tap Google review link, add QR codes at the gate, smart-lock kiosk and counter, and target 50+ reviews in 90 days to close the gap on the second and third pack slots. Highest-ROI move on the list. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
4
HIGH · LOCATION PAGES

No dedicated Vail, Avon, Edwards or Eagle location pages.

Impact: The whole valley is addressed from one homepage, so there is nothing indexable to rank for the up-valley commercial searches AAA Self Storage, StorageMart and Extra Space own.

The homepage does a good job describing airport-adjacency, climate control and smart locks, but it is a single page trying to rank for an entire valley. There are no separate, indexable landing pages for Gypsum, Vail, Avon, Edwards or Eagle. That is precisely why the up-valley terms (Avon #13 to #25, Eagle #22 to #23) underperform: the up-valley market is led by AAA Self Storage (storagevail.com), StorageMart and Extra Space, each of which has location-specific content you cannot match from one page. Your honest angle, the closer, cheaper, airport-adjacent down-valley option, needs its own pages to be searchable.

Fix: Build a lightweight, schema-rich landing page per area (Gypsum, Vail, Avon, Edwards, Eagle), each with a local intro, drive-time-from messaging, a unit table, amenities and an FAQ. Even 350-500 words each will outrank thin competitor pages on these long-tail local terms. Eta: 3-4 days.
5
HIGH · AI / GPT-5.5 GAP

GPT-5.5 is your weakest AI engine, dropping you on down-valley queries.

Impact: You are cited 17 of 20 overall, but GPT-5.5 cites you only 3 of 5 and omits you on the Vail Valley cost and Avon/Edwards prompts.

Across the four flagship models you are cited 17 of 20, an exceptional result: Gemini 5/5, Perplexity 5/5, Claude 4/5, but GPT-5.5 only 3/5. GPT-5.5 reliably cites you on airport, climate and Gypsum prompts, where your messaging is strongest, but drops you on "How much does a storage unit cost in the Vail Valley" and "Self storage near Edwards and Avon". On those down-valley questions it leans toward AAA Self Storage, StorageMart and Extra Space, exactly the up-valley players you lack dedicated pages for. The AI gap and the local-page gap are the same gap.

Fix: The location pages and a localized "storage cost in the Vail Valley" page in Findings 2 and 4 directly feed GPT-5.5 the quotable, area-specific text it is missing. Mirror the exact prompt phrasing in page H2s and FAQ answers. Eta: folds into the location-page work. Target: GPT-5.5 from 3/5 to 5/5.
Bottom line: this is an excellent website with a local-relevance gap. Unlike most audits, there is almost nothing to fix in the code, the schema, speed and structure are already done right. The wins are all about visibility: GBP, reviews, location pages and a keyword profile pointed at the valley instead of the country. Get those right and the perfect site finally wins the perfect map pack.
