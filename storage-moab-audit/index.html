02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild, and the technical foundation is excellent. Together they explain why an 18-of-20 AI darling still sits #6 organically.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · ON-PAGE METADATA

The homepage title is "Self Storage Units", with no "Moab" and no "Utah".

Impact: The single highest-leverage fix on the site. A generic title throws away the strongest on-page relevance signal for both Google and AI engines.

The live homepage <title> is literally "Self Storage Units", 18 characters, with zero geo-targeting. DataForSEO flags it title_too_short. The meta description ("Find secure self storage near you. View unit sizes, check availability, and rent online.") is equally generic and never says "Moab" or "Utah". On top of that there is no og:image (og:title/description/url are present, twitter:card=summary), so shares on Facebook, iMessage and WhatsApp render with no thumbnail. For a facility that is already cited by AI 18 of 20 times, the title is the cheapest, fastest relevance gain available.

Fix: Rewrite the title to e.g. Moab Self Storage | Drive-Up Storage Units in Moab, UT and the meta to a Moab/Arches-rich, benefit-led 150-character description. Add a branded 1200x630 og:image sitewide. Eta: 30 minutes. Expected lift: direct organic + AI relevance, working social previews.
2
CRITICAL · CONTENT & SCHEMA

A ~164-word homepage with no FAQPage schema caps organic upside.

Impact: Too thin to rank past competitors organically, and missing the FAQ markup that AI engines extract and quote verbatim.

The homepage runs only ~164 words, flagged by DataForSEO as low_content_rate + low_character_count. The good news, and it is genuinely rare, is that SelfStorage schema is already present with address, geo, hours and amenity features. The gap is two-fold: there is no FAQPage schema anywhere, and there is not enough prose for Google to understand the breadth of what you offer. FAQ markup answering cost, access and size questions in plain language is exactly the format Claude, Gemini, GPT and Sonar lift into their answers.

Fix: Expand the homepage to 500+ words covering drive-up access, paved lot, 24/7 entry, unit sizes and the Arches/Canyonlands gateway angle, and add a FAQPage block (snippet in Section 06) on the homepage and a dedicated /faq hub. Eta: 1-2 days copy + dev.
3
CRITICAL · PRICING TRANSPARENCY

No published pricing, and AI noticed.

Impact: Gemini dropped you on the "how much does storage cost in Moab" prompt because the site lists no rates; Perplexity surfaced a rival's prices instead.

The site publishes no unit prices. In our flagship AI test, Gemini 4.0 was the one model that did not cite Storage Moab on the cost prompt, while Perplexity answered the same question by quoting concrete rates ("5x10 drive-up around $88, 10x10 around $115") it pulled from elsewhere. There is also visible search demand for this exact intent: self storage moab utah prices and cheapest self storage moab utah both appear in "people also search". A published rate table is a small page change that closes the only structural gap in your AI coverage.

Fix: Add a published unit-size + rate table (5x5 through 10x30, drive-up) with month-to-month terms, plus an Offer/priceRange in schema and a "How much does storage cost in Moab?" FAQ answer. Eta: half a day. Expected lift: recovers the Gemini cost citation + the price-intent queries.
4
HIGH · ORGANIC RANK

Organic #6 / pack #3, behind Moab Valley Storage and A-1 Storage.

Impact: You are 3rd among real competitors despite the strongest AI presence in the market; reviews and content are the levers that close the gap.

On the live Moab SERP you sit at local pack #3 (behind A-1 Storage and Moab Storage) and organic #6 (behind Moab Valley Storage at #4 and A-1 at #5). Moab Valley Storage is the brand the AI engines repeatedly frame as "best overall", and A-1 is the strongest organically. The competitive set is small (about 8 real local rivals) and all of them run low-authority template or storage-software sites, so the door to the top 3 is wide open for whoever ships better metadata, deeper content and a steady review drip first.

Fix: Combine the metadata + content + pricing fixes above with a Google review drive (one-tap links at move-in, gate QR codes) to out-signal Moab Valley and A-1. Localize every title to Moab and interlink the new park-gateway pages. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
5
HIGH · UNTAPPED ANGLE

Nobody owns the "gateway to Arches & Canyonlands" angle, including you.

Impact: A high-intent, uncontested content lane (park visitors + outdoor-recreation gear) that Claude already showed it rewards, and where you currently lose.

Moab is the gateway to Arches and Canyonlands National Parks, and a real seasonal storage market exists around park visitors and outdoor gear (rafting, climbing, mountain biking, off-road). In our AI test, Claude was the one model that did not cite you on the "self storage near Arches National Park" prompt, losing the slot to vehicle-storage and other brands. No competitor fully owns this lane, and you mention drive-up + 24/7 + paved access but never the park-gateway or seasonal-gear use case. This is the clearest piece of green-field content in the market.

Fix: Publish a "Self Storage Near Arches & Canyonlands" gateway page plus a "Seasonal Storage for Moab Outdoor Gear" piece (rafting, climbing, mountain-bike, off-road), each with FAQ schema and the 1081 S Main St / Hwy 191 location framing. Eta: 2-3 days.
Bottom line: this is a strong business behind a generic on-page shell. Unlike most audits, the foundation (speed, Core Web Vitals, SelfStorage schema, AI trust) is already excellent. The wins here are surface-level, metadata, content depth, FAQ schema, published pricing and a park-gateway angle, and they are the difference between being the 18-of-20 runner-up and being the default first answer for storage in Moab.
