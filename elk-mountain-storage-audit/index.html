Executive Summary

The situation in 60 seconds.

Elk Mountain Storage is the convenient downtown choice in Crested Butte and is already cited in 12 of 20 flagship-AI answers, usually listed first when it appears. But the website ships zero schema markup, ranks for almost nothing beyond its own brand name, and trails on Google reviews. A short, mostly additive list of fixes would turn a clean, fast site into the default organic and AI answer for storage in Crested Butte.

  1. The site ships no schema markup at all. No SelfStorage, no LocalBusiness, no FAQPage (despite a live /faq page). For a single-location facility this is the single biggest missed signal for both the local pack and the AI engines, and it is the headline finding of this audit.
  2. AI already knows you, 12 of 20, and lists you first. Across Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.5 and Sonar-Reasoning-Pro, Elk Mountain Storage was cited 12 times in 20 calls and is almost always the first name when cited ("the only full-service storage facility in Downtown Crested Butte"). Claude cited you 5/5; GPT-5.5 (2/5) and Perplexity (1/5) are the weak spots.
  3. You rank for your own name and almost nothing else. Of ~16 ranked keywords, the only one you own is "elk mountain storage" (#1). You are absent from page 1 for any non-brand Crested Butte term, and roughly half the ranked set is out-of-market string-match noise (Banner Elk NC, Silt CO, Butte MT).
  4. The homepage is thin, ~562 words, and images have no alt text. A clean title, good meta, self-canonical and a single H1 are all in place, but there is not enough indexable depth to rank for the city's commercial terms.
  5. Most of the upside is unlocked by a handful of fixes, not a rebuild. Core Web Vitals already pass (LCP 1.9s, CLS 0.003). The gaps are schema, reviews, content depth, GPT/Perplexity AI visibility and on-page polish.

Our verdict

Elk Mountain Storage has done the hard part: it owns the brand, holds a downtown location no competitor can match, and loads fast on mobile. What it is missing is the structured-data layer, the review volume and the page depth that turn a convenient listing into the default organic and AI answer for storage in Crested Butte.

02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually
holding the site back.

Each finding below came from the live site, the Lighthouse audit, or the search data, not from theory. None requires a rebuild. The site is fast and clean; the gaps are structural and additive.

· · ·
1
CRITICAL · SCHEMA

The site ships zero schema markup. None.

Impact: Invisible to the structured-data signals that drive the local pack, rich results and AI citations, on a site where every other technical box is already ticked.

A full crawl found no JSON-LD of any kind on the homepage or interior pages: no SelfStorage, no LocalBusiness, no FAQPage (even though a real /faq page exists), no Product or Offer for unit sizes and prices. Everything else on the page is clean, the title, meta, canonical and H1 are all correct, which makes the total absence of structured data the single highest-leverage fix available. It is the reason GPT-5.5 and Perplexity, which lean on machine-readable signals and directories, repeatedly omit you while Claude (which parses raw text well) cites you 5 of 5.

Fix: Add a @graph with a SelfStorage / LocalBusiness node (NAP, geo, hours, priceRange, amenityFeature), a FAQPage on the homepage and /faq, and Offer/AggregateOffer for unit bands. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: 1 dev day. Expected lift: local-pack reinforcement, FAQ rich results, and AI-citation eligibility on GPT and Perplexity.
2
CRITICAL · REVIEWS

Only 5 Google reviews, the lowest count in the local pack.

Impact: Reviews drive both the local-pack ranking and the "best storage in Crested Butte" AI answers, where review volume decides ordering.

The listing shows a strong 4.8 rating from just 5 reviews, the fewest of the three facilities visible in the Crested Butte map pack. Review count is one of the deciding factors in Google's local ranking and in how Claude/GPT/Gemini order their "best storage" lists; they tend to lead with the most-reviewed, most-trusted facility. You have the rating and a strong downtown story, you are missing the volume of social proof to convert that into a higher pack position and steadier AI inclusion.

Fix: Launch a review drive: SMS/email every move-in with a one-tap Google review link, add a QR code at the gate and the counter, and ask in person at lease signing. Target 25+ reviews in 90 days. This is among the highest-ROI moves on the entire list. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
3
CRITICAL · ORGANIC FOOTPRINT

You rank for your brand and almost nothing else, no city landing pages, thin content.

Impact: ~16 ranked keywords, only the brand term in the top 10; absent from page 1 for every commercial Crested Butte query.

DataForSEO Labs shows the only money keyword you own is your own brand, elk mountain storage (#1, vol 30). The high-intent query self storage crested butte co is not in your ranked set organically at all, you appear only in the map pack. Roughly half the ranked list is accidental out-of-market string matching ("Banner Elk NC", "Elk Vale" in South Dakota, "Storage Solutions of Butte" in Montana, "Silt CO"), not real local demand. With a ~562-word homepage (low-content flag) and no dedicated pages for "storage units crested butte", RV/boat or drive-up storage, there simply is not enough indexable depth to rank for the town's commercial terms.

Fix: Build keyword-targeted, schema-rich landing pages: /storage-units-crested-butte-co, /rv-boat-storage-crested-butte, /drive-up-storage-crested-butte, plus expand the homepage past 600 words with a unit table and FAQ. Eta: 3-4 days copy + dev.
4
HIGH · AI VISIBILITY

GPT-5.5 and Perplexity drop you on transactional queries.

Impact: Claude 5/5 and Gemini 4/5, but GPT-5.5 only 2/5 and Perplexity only 1/5, exactly the engines behind ChatGPT and Perplexity search.

The AI test makes the split clear. Claude (5/5) and Gemini (4/5) consistently name Elk Mountain, often first, as "the only full-service storage facility in Downtown Crested Butte." But GPT-5.5 cited you only twice and Perplexity only once (the business-storage prompt). On the rent/cost/RV prompts, GPT and Perplexity lean on aggregators (Storagefront, RentCafe) and competing facilities and omit you. The pattern lines up with the schema gap and the lack of on-page pricing/RV pages: the engines that need machine-readable, directory-backed signals can not find you.

Fix: Ship the schema in Section 06, publish on-page pricing and a dedicated RV/boat page (the /business-storage page already earned the Perplexity citation, replicate its depth), and strengthen third-party listings (Yelp, Storagefront, RentCafe, BBB) so GPT and Perplexity have a source to ground. Eta: aligned with schema + content sprints.
5
HIGH · ON-PAGE / A11Y

Homepage images carry no alt text, and content is too thin to rank.

Impact: Accessibility and image-search signals lost; ~562-word homepage limits topical authority for a visual, local business.

The crawl flagged no_image_alt = true, images on the homepage ship without alt attributes, costing accessibility points (a11y already 0.90, easy to lift) and image-search visibility for a business whose units and downtown location are inherently visual. The page also runs only ~562 words with a low-content flag, and carries 5 render-blocking scripts plus 9 render-blocking stylesheets that, while not yet hurting Core Web Vitals, are worth trimming on the Duda/Duos.io theme.

Fix: Add descriptive, location-specific alt text to every image (e.g. "drive-up storage units at 307 Red Lady Ave, downtown Crested Butte CO"), expand the homepage past 600 words with a unit table and amenities, and remove the duplicate google-site-verification meta tag the parser flagged. Eta: 3-4 hours.
Bottom line: this is a fast, clean site that is structurally invisible. Unlike most audits, the foundation (title, meta, canonical, H1, Core Web Vitals) is already solid, Core Web Vitals actually pass. The wins here are additive: schema, reviews, content depth, and a lift on GPT and Perplexity. They are the difference between being the convenient downtown option AI mentions and being the default answer every engine and the map pack lead with.

The gap is coverage, not reputation. Why this matters now

AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews) is displacing a growing share of "near me" storage discovery, and in a resort town with seasonal and second-home buyers, that share is high. The four models tested are exactly what powers those surfaces. You are already the first name on two of them, the risk is staying invisible on ChatGPT and Perplexity while the Gunnison operators are not.

What earns citations

  1. SelfStorage + LocalBusiness schema, the machine-readable signal you ship none of today.
  2. FAQPage schema answering cost, access and RV questions in plain prose, the format LLMs extract.
  3. On-page pricing + Offer schema so the "how much" and "cheap" prompts have a source on your domain, not an aggregator.
  4. An RV/boat page matching the depth of the /business-storage page that already earned the Perplexity citation.
  5. Review volume past the pack, AI lists lead with the most-reviewed, most-trusted facility.
  6. Third-party listings (Yelp, Storagefront, RentCafe, BBB) so GPT and Perplexity find you where they already look.
