01 · Executive Summary

The 60-second verdict.

Good news first, because there is a lot of it: the foundation you paid for is genuinely strong. The problem is narrow, specific, and fixable, and it is not the website's engine.

  • What is going great. Impressions grew 52% month over month, the Spanish pages convert at 2.03% CTR (16x the English pages and the single best-performing thing on the whole site), Lighthouse is 98/100, and AI assistants already cite you for floor stripping & waxing and post-construction cleaning. The team is right: SEO and your Google Business Profile are bringing in real leads.
  • What is not. Click-through rate has fallen three months straight (0.44% → 0.30% → 0.18%). Average position slid from 23.5 to 37 in June. You rank for thousands of terms but converted them into only 20 organic clicks in the last 28 days. The site is visible, not clickable.
  • The single root cause. Intent mismatch. Most of your English impressions come from residential-sounding searches ("hardwood floor cleaning davie fl", "carpet cleaning hialeah") that your titles immediately disqualify with the word "Commercial." Real impressions, wrong audience, no click.
  • The fastest win. You already rank, so rewriting titles, metas and schema to pre-qualify the searcher and earn rich-result stars converts existing rankings into clicks within weeks, no new authority required.
  • The biggest growth lever. Off-page, not on-page. Page 1 for every money term is a Google Map pack plus national franchises. Winning means Google Business Profile, reviews, citations and a handful of links, plus doubling down on the verticals you already win in AI answers.

04 · The Five Critical Findings

Five things between you
and a lead machine.

Ranked by impact on lead flow, not by effort. The first three are about converting the visibility you already have. The last two are about widening it.

· · ·
1
P0 · Critical

You rank, but nobody clicks: the CTR collapse

CTR 0.44% → 0.30% → 0.18% over three months · 33,600 impressions → 20 clicks in 28 days

This is the headline wound. Google shows your pages tens of thousands of times and almost nobody clicks. A 0.18% CTR at an average position of 29 is roughly one-tenth of what those positions should earn. The cause is not ranking, it is the snippet: titles run 80 to 92 characters (truncated in the SERP), value props get cut off, and rich-result stars are missing on key pages. You are leaving the clicks you already earned on the table.

Fix: Rewrite every template title to fit ~58 characters with the hook up front (city + "Commercial" + price or "Free Quote"), tighten metas to ~155 characters ending in the phone number, and roll AggregateRating + Service + FAQ schema across all ranking templates so stars show. Full rewrites in Quick Wins and the URL Catalog.
2
P0 · Critical

Intent mismatch: you rank for searches you cannot serve

Most English impressions are residential-intent · you are commercial-only

The deeper reason behind the low CTR. Your biggest impression generators are queries like "hardwood floor cleaning davie fl" (position 4.35, zero clicks), "carpet cleaning hialeah", and "tile cleaning miami", searches dominated by homeowners. Your titles and metas correctly lead with "Commercial", so the homeowner reads the snippet, realizes you are not for them, and scrolls on. The impressions are real; the audience is wrong. You are paying for visibility you can never convert.

Fix: Bake the qualifier into the title so the wrong searcher self-filters before they cost you a CTR-depressing impression, and the right one knows instantly they found their match: Commercial Carpet Cleaning Hialeah, Offices & Retail | From $0.30/sq ft. Lean your programmatic engine toward the terms a facilities manager actually types ("commercial", "office", "warehouse", "post-construction"), not the generic ones homeowners share.
3
P1 · High

The /areas/ hubs are impression black holes

24 area pages · 10,117 impressions · 2 clicks · 0.02% CTR

Your city hub pages (/areas/delray-beach, /areas/boynton-beach, and 22 more) vacuum up head-term impressions for "commercial cleaning [city]" but rank on page 3 to 6 and almost never get clicked. Three compounding problems: a template bug renders the brand name twice in the title ("...| MB Clean Solutions | MB Clean Solutions"), the pages are thin near-duplicates (~464 words, zero images, no FAQ schema), and the titles carry no price or hook. To Google they read as low-value doorways, which is also a ranking risk.

Fix: Kill the duplicate-brand bug today (a one-line template fix on ~24 pages). Then turn each hub into a real local landing page: 700+ words of city-specific proof, 2 to 3 photos, an FAQ block with FAQPage schema, internal links to the services you perform there, and a title like Commercial Cleaning Delray Beach FL | Free On-Site Quote.
4
P1 · High

Commercially invisible where it pays: the local pack and the money terms

0 keywords on page 1 · "commercial cleaning miami" sits at position 37 to 51

For every commercial money term, page 1 is split between a Google Map pack of local listings (competitors carry 50 to 139 reviews) and national franchises (Jani-King, JAN-PRO, Anago, Vanguard, Office Pride, Stratus). You appear in neither. Your service pages for "miami commercial cleaning" and "commercial cleaning company miami" already exist, they just sit on page 4 to 7 for lack of authority and local signals. The gap is off-page, not on-page.

Fix: Win the map pack first, it is half the real estate and the cheapest lead source you have. Optimize the Google Business Profile, run a review-generation cadence (target 40+ reviews in 90 days), and build local citations (BBB, Yelp, Angi, chambers). Then close the link gap on the head terms with a handful of local and industry links. Detail in Backlinks.
5
P1 · Opportunity

Two untapped engines: the AI wedge and the missing vertical pages

AI cites you for floor care & post-construction · zero pages for high-volume niches

You already win in AI answers for floor stripping & waxing and post-construction, because those pages carry exactly what an LLM extracts: per-square-foot pricing, process steps, certifications, NAP. Replicate that formula and you own a category most competitors have not noticed. Separately, Semrush surfaced high-volume, low-difficulty niches you have no page for at all: auto dealership cleaning (2,400/mo, KD 8), property management cleaning (590/mo, KD 6), medical office cleaning (KD 4).

Fix: Standardize the "citable page" formula (price + process + certifications + NAP + same-day) across every service and city, and clean up the brand confusion between mbcleansolutions.com and the sister mbfloorsolutions.com. Then build deep pages for the four missing verticals. Detail in Content Strategy and AI Visibility.

To compare fairly we rebuilt true 28-day windows from the daily Chart series rather than comparing unequal totals. Queries, Pages, Devices, Countries, and the daily chart were all parsed.

Live crawl: Playwright, 12 page templates, fully scrolled, desktop 1440px plus mobile and tablet on the homepage, June 28. Extracted titles, metas, canonicals, H1 to H4, JSON-LD types, image alt coverage, word counts, analytics tags.

Rankings & competition: DataForSEO (ranked keywords, SERP ownership, keyword volumes, Lighthouse) + Semrush (Authority Score, organic footprint, keyword gap, backlinks, anchors).

AI visibility: DataForSEO LLM Responses API, 5 prompts x 4 flagship models with web search enabled, 20 calls total.
