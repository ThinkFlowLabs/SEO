CRITICAL · AI VISIBILITY

GPT-5.5 cites you 0 of 5 times, and it is the highest-traffic AI assistant.

Impact: ChatGPT, the most-used AI search surface, never names Slate River. It defaults to CB Self Storage and other operators in every Crested Butte storage prompt.

Fix: Earn the corroborating web presence GPT rewards: claim and complete authoritative directory and citation listings with identical NAP, add quotable FAQ and pricing prose the model can extract, and grow third-party mentions (local press, chamber, partner pages). Snippet targets in Section 06 and 08. Eta: ongoing, start week 1. KPI: GPT-5.5 from 0/5 to 3+/5.
2
CRITICAL · OFF-PAGE AUTHORITY

Only ~2 referring domains, and no top-20 organic rank for your core term.

Impact: You hold the local pack at #2 but have no organic page-one presence for "self storage crested butte co", so you depend entirely on the map.

DataForSEO shows roughly 2 referring domains and 5 backlinks total, an anemic profile even for a small market. The consequence is direct: the site does not rank in the top 20 organically for its money term and relies on the Google local pack (#2) for visibility. The few high-volume terms the tools report as "ranked" ("slate storage", "slate storage rocky river") are vanity matches to unrelated out-of-state "Slate Storage" brands, not real Crested Butte traffic. In a market this small you do not need many links; you need the right local citations, all with identical name, address and phone.

Fix: Build 8 to 12 high-quality local citations and partnership links (chamber, regional directories, movers, realtors, RV/ski outfitters), every one with identical NAP. Pair with the content pages in Section 07 so there is something link-worthy to point at. Eta: weeks 1-8, ongoing. Target: 12+ referring domains, top-3 organic for the head term.
3
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

A 577-word homepage with no dedicated location or service pages.

Impact: Too thin to rank organically and nothing for AI engines to quote on price, drive-up, RV or Gunnison-Valley intent.

The homepage runs about 577 visible words and does the work of the entire site. There are no dedicated, indexable pages for the obvious modifiers, drive-up storage, RV/boat/vehicle storage, the Gunnison Valley service area, a storage size guide, or a pricing explainer. Third-party operators and aggregators (Yelp, Storagefront, CB Self Storage) take page-one organic slots precisely because they publish deeper, more structured content about Crested Butte storage than you do about your own facility. This thinness is also why GPT-5.5 has little of yours to corroborate and quote.

Fix: Build a small set of schema-rich landing pages, drive-up storage, RV/boat/vehicle, a Gunnison Valley service-area page, a size guide and a cost explainer, and expand the homepage past 800 words with a unit table, amenities and links to those pages. Eta: 3-5 days copy + dev.
4
HIGH · CORE WEB VITALS

Mobile LCP is 2.9s, the one weak vital, capping Performance at 0.82.

Impact: LCP scores just 0.35 in Lighthouse and holds mobile Performance at 0.82; everything else in the audit is green.

DataForSEO Lighthouse (mobile): Performance 0.82, SEO 0.92, Accessibility 0.90, Best Practices 1.0. The one drag is LCP at 2.9s (score 0.35; Google's "Good" threshold is <2.5s). Crucially, CLS is a near-perfect 0.008, so there is no layout-shift problem to solve, the fix is narrow and almost entirely about the hero/LCP image. Get LCP under 2.5s and Performance clears 0.90 with no other CWV work needed.

Fix: Serve the hero/LCP image at the right dimensions with explicit width/height + fetchpriority="high", preload it, and use a modern format (AVIF/WebP) sized for mobile. The og:image is a 1920w PNG, so the LCP asset is likely oversized. Eta: half a day. Target: LCP < 2.5s, Perf > 0.90.
5
HIGH · REVIEW VELOCITY

You lead the pack on reviews, 6 at 5.0, but the lead is thin and beatable.

Impact: Reviews are your single strongest differentiator and a top driver of both local-pack order and "best storage" AI answers, but 6 reviews is a fragile lead.

Slate River holds a 5.0 rating from 6 reviews, the most of any facility in its local pack, and Perplexity singled you out as "best-reviewed, most established." That is the reason three of four AI models cite you at all. But six reviews is a slim moat: a competitor running a review drive could overtake it in a month, and more reviews would push you from "listed second or third" toward "listed first" in the AI answers. This is the cheapest lever you have, and you are already winning it, press the advantage.

Fix: Launch a structured Google review drive: one-tap review link in every move-in SMS/email, a QR code at the gate and counter, and a target of 25+ reviews while holding the 5.0. Surface review snippets on the homepage and location pages so AI engines can quote them. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
Bottom line: this is a well-built business held back off the page. Unlike most audits, the technical foundation, schema, canonical, og:image, CLS, is already done right. The wins here are reach: earn your way into GPT-5.5, build local authority, deepen the content, tune one image, and keep extending your review lead. That is the difference between owning the map and owning the entire search result.
