02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's quietly

1
CRITICAL · KEYWORD FOCUS

Your highest-volume rankings are off-topic camping content, not RV storage.

Impact: A legacy travel-content block draws volume with zero storage intent, while the real RV-storage money terms sit on page 2-3.

Of the 40 ranked keywords DataForSEO returned, the highest-volume ones are a block of Dillon Reservoir / Lake Dillon camping / Pine Cove campground queries ("dillon reservoir" alone is 12,100/mo) from what looks like an old blog or resources page. They rank weakly (positions 14-54) and convert nobody, RV-storage buyers do not search for campgrounds. Meanwhile your actual money terms (rv covered storage 2,900/mo at ~37, storage in gypsum co 260/mo at ~22, self storage eagle co 320/mo at ~28) all sit on page 2-3. Only ~12 of 40 ranked terms are genuinely in-market.

Fix: Prune or 301-redirect the Dillon/camping content to the most relevant storage page, then build dedicated, keyword-targeted landing pages for the in-market terms (covered RV storage, gypsum/eagle storage, car & boat storage). Eta: 1 day prune + 3-4 days pages. Expected lift: move money terms from page 2-3 into the top 5 and stop diluting topical focus.
2
CRITICAL · SCHEMA

No FAQPage schema, so the cost and where-to-store PAA goes uncaptured.

Impact: FAQ markup is the format LLMs and Google rich results quote directly; its absence leaves the exact questions you already rank for on the table.

Good news first: the site already ships SelfStorage (a LocalBusiness subtype) and a full og:image / Twitter card, so the core structured-data and social layers are healthy. What is missing is any FAQPage markup. The live People-Also-Ask box for your terms is full of high-intent questions, "how much does RV storage cost near Vail", "where can I store an RV in the Vail Valley", "is there heated RV storage near Eagle County Airport", which are exactly the prompts the AI models are already answering with your name. FAQ schema turns those into rich-result and AI-quotable assets.

Fix: Add a FAQPage block to the homepage and the crown-jewel landing pages, seeded with the cost / where-to-store / access questions from Section 07. Snippet in Section 06. Eta: half a dev day. Expected lift: FAQ rich results + reinforced AI citations on the cost and where-to prompts.
3
HIGH · ON-PAGE HOUSEKEEPING

Thin ~406-word body, a stray-space H1 bug, 12 images with no alt, 9 HTML errors.

Impact: A cluster of small validity and content issues that each shave a little off an otherwise excellent (95.24 on-page) site.

The homepage body runs only ~406 words (flagged low-content for a page meant to anchor a niche). The single H1 renders with a stray space, "Covere d RV Storage Near Eagle Vail Airport", almost certainly an inline tag splitting the word. All 12 images are missing alt and title attributes (an accessibility and image-search cost for an inherently visual business), there are 9 HTML validity errors (invalid attribute-name characters on two lines plus one malformed comment), and a duplicate google-site-verification meta tag.

Fix: Fix the H1 markup so it reads "Covered" cleanly, add descriptive location-specific alt text to all 12 images (e.g. "covered RV storage canopy at 645 Lindbergh Dr, Gypsum CO"), resolve the 9 validity errors, remove the duplicate verification tag, and expand the homepage body to 600+ words. Eta: 3-4 hours.
4
HIGH · REVIEWS

14 Google reviews (4.7★) while New Frontier has 34.

Impact: Reviews are the one local signal where a rival leads you; they reinforce both the local-pack order and the "best storage" AI lists.

Eagle Canopy holds a strong 4.7★ from 14 reviews, but New Frontier Self Storage in Gypsum sits at 34. You currently lead the local pack and the AI answers anyway, so this is not a fire, it is the cheapest insurance you can buy. In a market this size review volume is the deciding factor if a competitor ever closes the gap on schema and content, and it is one of the inputs the LLMs lean on when ordering "best storage" recommendations.

Fix: Launch a review drive: a one-tap Google review link in every move-in confirmation, a QR code at the gate and the office, and a target of 35+ reviews to clear New Frontier. Eta: ongoing, start week 1. Highest-ROI off-site move on the list.
5
HIGH · CROWN-JEWEL PAGES

The deep pages the LLMs cite are unprotected and under-linked.

Impact: /rv-storage-near-vail and /covered-rv-storage-colorado are the assets driving the 20/20 sweep; losing or thinning them would put the lead at risk.

The AI models repeatedly cite two deep pages, /rv-storage-near-vail and /covered-rv-storage-colorado, as the source for "closest covered RV storage to Vail" and "most specialized option". These are the crown jewels of the whole site, and yet they live somewhat in isolation. They deserve to be protected (never pruned or merged), expanded with the cost / amenity / FAQ detail the models love to quote, and tied into the rest of the site with clear internal links so their authority compounds rather than leaks.

Fix: Treat both pages as protected assets: expand each with a unit/price table, amenity list and FAQ block, add internal links to them from the homepage and the new storage pages, and keep their URLs stable. Eta: 1-2 days. Expected lift: durable AI citations + recovered organic rank for the broad RV terms.
Bottom line: this is an exceptional presence on a tidy-needs-doing website. Unlike most audits, the headline result is a clean sweep, 20 of 20 AI citations and the #1 local slot. The wins here are protective and additive: refocus the keywords, add FAQ schema, clean the HTML, grow reviews and fortify the crown-jewel pages. The job is to defend and widen a lead, not to claw one back.
