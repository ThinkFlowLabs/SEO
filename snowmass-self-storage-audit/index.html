Executive Summary

The situation in 60 seconds.

Snowmass Self Storage has built something most small storage facilities never achieve: it is cited in 16 of 20 flagship-AI answers, almost always as the #1 recommendation, and its technical foundation (rich schema, fast Core Web Vitals) is genuinely excellent. The problem is the opposite of the usual audit. The site is almost completely absent from classic organic search in the Aspen corridor it serves. It ranks #1 for its own name and essentially nowhere else.

  1. You already win in AI, but you are invisible in organic. Across Claude, Gemini, GPT-5.5 and Sonar you were cited 16 of 20 times and usually listed #1 for Snowmass-specific prompts. Yet for the money keyword "self storage aspen co" your own domain does not appear in the mobile SERP at all, you surface only through the Aspen Chamber directory and a Yelp list.
  2. You rank #1 organically for exactly one thing: your own brand name. DataForSEO shows only ~14 ranked keywords. "aspen storage units" sits at #42, "aspen self storage" at #22, and the high-intent local pack is owned end-to-end by Aspen Self Storage Warehouses, Low Cost Storage Aspen and Aspen Secure Storage.
  3. Your technical and structured-data work is already done, and done well. The site ships full SelfStorage schema (geo, hours, 9 amenities), an OfferCatalog with 15 priced units, and a 12-question FAQPage. Lighthouse is Perf 0.89, LCP 1.9s, CLS 0.002, all Core Web Vitals pass. This is why AI cites you. It is not the problem.
  4. Competitors show reviews; you show none in the SERP. Aspen Self Storage Warehouses surfaces at 4.8 from 38 reviews in the local pack. Snowmass Self Storage shows no rating at all, which costs you both the local pack and the "best storage in Aspen" AI answers as queries broaden.
  5. The fix is content and corridor pages, not a rebuild. A handful of dedicated Aspen, Basalt, Carbondale and Roaring Fork Valley pages, a pricing/cost page, a review drive and GBP optimization would convert your AI dominance into matching organic and local-pack visibility.

Our verdict

Snowmass Self Storage has done the hard, technical part that almost every competitor skips: clean schema, priced offers, a real FAQ, and a fast site. That is why four frontier AI models already name you first for Snowmass storage. What you have not done is publish the first-party organic content that wins the Aspen corridor on Google, so a strong brand is leaking the entire non-brand market to rivals and directories.

Optimize the Google Business Profile and launch a review drive in 30 days. Build the Aspen / Basalt / Carbondale corridor and cost pages in 60 days. Earn the local citations and re-measure in 90 days. We project Snowmass Self Storage can move from invisible to a top-5 organic baseline for non-brand Aspen terms, into the Aspen local pack, and from 16 of 20 to 19+ of 20 AI citations that hold even as queries broaden to "Roaring Fork Valley".

02 · The Five Critical Findings

What's actually

1
CRITICAL · ORGANIC VISIBILITY

Absent from the "self storage aspen co" organic results, ranks #1 only for its own brand.

Impact: The highest-intent commercial term in your market does not return your domain at all. Competitors and directories take 100% of the non-brand click.

For "self storage aspen co" on mobile, snowmassselfstorage.com does not appear anywhere in the SERP. You surface only indirectly, through the Aspen Chamber business directory (rank ~#13) and as a name inside a Yelp "Best 10" list. DataForSEO Labs confirms it: only ~14 ranked keywords, and the only top-10 organic ranking is for the brand term "snowmass self storage" (#1). Non-brand intent goes to Aspen Self Storage Warehouses (organic #1 + local pack #1), Low Cost Storage Aspen, All Hours Storage and the aggregators (Sparefoot, SelfStorage.com).

Fix: Build first-party, indexable corridor landing pages, one per town you serve (Aspen, Basalt, Carbondale, Glenwood, Woody Creek), each with its own copy, unit table, map, hours and FAQ. Internally link them from the homepage. This is the single biggest organic lever you have, and it is pure greenfield. Eta: 1-2 weeks copy + dev.
2
CRITICAL · REVIEWS / GBP

No Google rating surfaces in the SERP. Your top rival shows 4.8 from 38 reviews.

Impact: Reviews drive the local pack and the "best storage in Aspen" AI answers, the exact place your citations fade as queries broaden.

In the Aspen local pack, Aspen Self Storage Warehouses shows 4.8 stars from 38 reviews; Snowmass Self Storage surfaces no rating at all. In a corridor this size, review count is the deciding factor in both Google's local ranking and the way Claude, GPT, Gemini and Sonar order their "best storage near Aspen" lists, they lead with the most-reviewed facility. You have the AI mindshare; you are missing the social proof that lets it hold up when the query says "Aspen" instead of "Snowmass".

Fix: Optimize/verify the Google Business Profile (categories, photos, services, Q&A, both Snowmass and Aspen service areas) and launch a review drive: one-tap Google review links at move-in by SMS/email, a QR code at the gate and counter. Target 25+ reviews in 90 days. Highest-ROI off-site move on the list. Eta: ongoing, start week 1.
3
CRITICAL · AI CORRIDOR DECAY

AI citations are strong for "Snowmass" but decay as queries broaden to Aspen / Roaring Fork.

Impact: You are cited 4/4 on Snowmass prompts but only 2/4 on "Roaring Fork Valley" and 3/4 on "near Aspen" / cost, the broader, higher-volume queries.

The 5x4 AI grid is clear: on "Best self storage in Snowmass" all four models cite you, usually #1. But on "Self storage in the Roaring Fork Valley" only Claude and GPT-5.5 cite you (Gemini and Sonar drop you), and on cost prompts you slip to 3/4. The reason is that your on-page copy and schema are anchored almost entirely to "Snowmass"; the models have less first-party Aspen / Basalt / Carbondale / pricing text to ground a broader recommendation, so they fall back to competitors like Aspen Self Storage Warehouses and All Hours Storage.

Fix: Publish corridor + pricing pages (Aspen, Basalt, Carbondale, Roaring Fork Valley, "how much does storage cost near Aspen") with quotable, plain-prose answers and matching FAQ schema. This is the exact text the dropping models need to keep citing you. Eta: part of the corridor page build.
4
HIGH · CONTENT DEPTH

The site is too thin to rank organically, with one ~441-word page and no corridor or pricing pages.

Impact: ~441 words total, ~14 ranked keywords, no dedicated Aspen / Basalt / Carbondale / Roaring Fork / pricing pages to capture corridor intent.

The homepage runs roughly 441 words and is effectively the whole site. There is no indexable landing page per town in the service area (Aspen, Snowmass, Woody Creek, Basalt, Carbondale, Glenwood Springs), and no page targeting the obvious modifiers (pricing/cost, RV/vehicle, climate, business storage). Directories like the Aspen Chamber and Yelp outrank you for your own market precisely because they publish more structured corridor content about storage than your own site does.

Fix: Add a real landing page per corridor town with address context, map, hours, unit table, amenities and FAQ, plus a "how much does storage cost near Aspen / Snowmass" pricing hub. Expand the homepage past 600 words and link out to the new pages. Eta: 1-2 weeks copy + dev.
5
HIGH · LOCAL PACK

Absent from the Aspen local pack while three rivals own all three slots.

Impact: The map pack for "self storage aspen co" is held end-to-end by competitors; you appear on no map surface for the corridor's head term.

The local pack for "self storage aspen co" is Aspen Self Storage Warehouses, Low Cost Storage Aspen and Aspen Secure Storage, all three slots. Snowmass Self Storage does not appear. For a facility on Hwy 82 that explicitly serves Aspen, this is a pure GBP and proximity-signal gap: the profile needs Aspen-area service areas, correct primary category, photos and reviews so Google associates you with the corridor, not only the Old Snowmass pin.

Fix: Optimize the GBP with Aspen and Roaring Fork service areas, the correct primary category (Self-storage facility), 15+ geotagged photos, services with prices, and a steady review flow. Pair with the corridor landing pages so the on-site signal matches the profile. Eta: half day + ongoing.
Bottom line: this is the inverse of a typical audit. The technical and AI foundations are already excellent, rich schema, fast Core Web Vitals, 16-of-20 AI citations. The gap is entirely classic organic and local: first-party corridor pages, reviews and a GBP tuned to Aspen. Closing it converts a facility that AI already names first into one that also wins Google.
